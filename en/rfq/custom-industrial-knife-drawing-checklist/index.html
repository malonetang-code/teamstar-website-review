ENGINEERING RFQ GUIDE

Industrial machine knife RFQ preparation guide

A complete drawing is useful but not mandatory. A physical sample, old-blade photographs or documented machine and operating conditions can also start a manufacturing review.

Submit a technical RFQ Review preparation routes
STARTING POINT

Choose the route that matches the available information

The three routes lead to the same manual technical review. Unknown items can be identified and confirmed after the enquiry is received.

01 DRAWING

Drawing-based manufacture

Use when a controlled drawing, CAD file or dimensional sketch is available.

Primary reference
Drawing number, revision and units
Key details
Overall dimensions, thickness, mounting interfaces and controlled tolerances
Also include
Processed material, machine information, quantity and current issue
Start from a drawing→
02 SAMPLE

Physical-sample replication

Use when an old blade, unused sample or equipment-specific part is available.

Primary reference
Physical sample and photographs of both sides
Key details
Mounting position, edge condition, wear pattern and known dimensions
Also include
Machine make and model, processed material and sample availability
Start from a sample→
03 APPLICATION

Application review

Use when the requirement begins with a machine, processed material or cutting problem.

Primary reference
Machine make, model and operating method
Key details
Processed material, mounting method, operating frequency and current failure
Also include
Installation-space dimensions, photographs, quantity and target service life
Start from an application→
DRAWING CHECKLIST

Information to show on a technical drawing

Mark the characteristics that control fit, cutting performance and inspection. Do not add tolerances or material requirements that have not been technically confirmed.

  1. 01
    Overall geometryLength or outside diameter, width, thickness and profile dimensions
  2. 02
    Mounting interfacesBores, slots, countersinks, pitch dimensions and reference datums
  3. 03
    Edge definitionCutting-edge position, bevel direction and controlled edge geometry
  4. 04
    Controlled requirementsApplicable tolerances, material or hardness requirements and surface requirements
  5. 05
    Document controlDrawing number, revision, units, quantity and approval status
WITHOUT A DRAWING

Photographs and measurements that support initial review

Full bladeFront, back and edge views with a scale reference
Mounting areaBores, slots, shoulders, mating parts and installation orientation
Machine identificationMachine nameplate, make, model and blade location
Wear and failureClose photographs of wear, chipping, deformation or cut-quality problems

MANUAL ENGINEERING REVIEW

What happens after submission

Quotation follows technical review. Geometry, material, heat treatment, machining, grinding and inspection requirements can each change the process route and price.

01RFQ registrationThe enquiry and uploaded files are assigned an independent RFQ reference
02Information reviewEngineering checks the manufacturing references and identifies missing controlled information
03Technical clarificationQuestions on geometry, application, acceptance or sample validation are confirmed
04Process and quotationThe applicable process route, price and lead time are prepared after manual review
Why Teamstar does not provide instant automated pricing

A single manufacturing or inspection requirement can materially change the process route and total cost. Manual review keeps the quotation aligned with the actual blade geometry, application and acceptance requirements.
